The ingredient case

FOCUS contains four active ingredients. The combination is clinically coherent. Evidence varies by ingredient — this section is precise about what can and cannot be claimed.

09
Slide 10 of 17
Section 3 — Ingredients
What the formulation does
Two mechanisms, one coherent outcome.
Primary focus mechanism

L-Theanine

Increases alpha brainwave activity — the brain state associated with wakeful relaxation and sustained attention. Multiple EEG studies confirm the effect. Described as "calm focus": alert but not anxious.

Evidence: double-blind RCT in 34 adults under cognitive stress. Alpha brainwave activity well-documented. Claims should be framed as "supports" not "improves." (ALZDISCOVERY; Frontiers in Nutrition, 2024)

Stress interference layer

Lactium®

Reduces cortisol levels in stressed adults. In a cognitive context, this matters because elevated cortisol degrades attention, working memory, and decision-making. Lactium creates the conditions for L-Theanine to work.

Evidence: 9 clinical studies, 500+ healthy volunteers. FDA-authorised stress-reduction claims. 30-day intervention typical. (Ingredia, 2020; Scientific Wellness)

Quality foundation

Lactoferrin (Proferrin®)

Neuroprotective properties documented — iron regulation, anti-inflammatory effect at the blood-brain barrier. Long-term and preventive rather than acute. In FOCUS communications, positioned as the brand's signature ingredient and quality marker.

Evidence base is for neuroprotection, not acute cognitive performance. Do not anchor the cognitive claim here.

Foundational support

Vitamin B12

Supports normal neurological function and energy metabolism. Credible but not differentiating — widely used in cognitive supplements. Its role in FOCUS is foundational support, not mechanism of action.

Well-established. Completes the formulation. Not the claim anchor.

Section 4 — Positioning
The FOCUS vs SNOOZE question
Same biology. Different application window. That's the resolution.

Both FOCUS and SNOOZE contain Lactoferrin and Lactium. On the surface: a contradiction. One product for clarity, one for sleep, sharing core ingredients. The resolution is context and timing, not mechanism.

FOCUS — Morning & daytime

Lactium reduces cortisol. Lower cortisol in the morning means less cognitive interference — you think more clearly. L-Theanine sustains the attentional state.

Same
biology
↕

SNOOZE — Evening

Lactium reduces cortisol. Lower cortisol in the evening means easier transition to sleep. The mechanism is identical. The application window is different.

This framing is honest, coherent, and defensible. It also opens a whole-day narrative: IMMUNE for resilience, FOCUS for the working day, SNOOZE for recovery. Not a product-by-product argument — a brand architecture story.

Section 4 — Positioning
Proposed positioning direction
Stay sharp. Not because the day demands it — because you do.
The positioning FOCUS is for people who are already performing well and want to maintain the quality of their thinking — through demanding days, without stimulants, without compromise.
The customer High-functioning adults — 28–55 — who are deliberate about their health, pay attention to how they think, and choose ingredients they understand. They are not looking for a fix. They are protecting something they value.

Commercial priorities

Three parallel workstreams for H2 2026. Each addresses a different constraint. They run concurrently — but the order of resolution matters.

15
Slide 16 of 17 — Part A
Section 5 — Commercial
Three workstreams
Subscription conversion, FOC-015 restructure, standalone acquisition.
1

Subscription conversion

329 customers bought once and stopped. Subscribers spend 2× more. The reorder interval is 30 days — the cadence is there. Audit the post-purchase sequence. Introduce FOC-090 subscription as the primary upsell. Test a trial-to-subscribe path for FOC-030. Day-25 email timed to anticipated reorder.

2

FOC-015 restructure

93 orders, £33 AOV, minimum price of £0. No evidence FOC-015 buyers convert to larger variants. Option A: retire as standalone, use as gifting/seeding tool. Option B: redesign as an explicit trial SKU with a designed conversion path. Decision gates the acquisition approach.

3

Standalone acquisition

Paid search test on 2–3 high-intent queries around cognitive clarity and clean performance. One long-form ingredient article for search and AI citation. IMMUNE customer email — introduce FOCUS as the deliberate complement for the working day, to subscribers who haven't yet bought it.

Priority order matters. Subscription conversion first — it works on the existing base with no new acquisition cost. FOC-015 decision second — it determines what the entry product is. Standalone acquisition third — don't point paid media at FOCUS until the entry product and conversion path are resolved.
