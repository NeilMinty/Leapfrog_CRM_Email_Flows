Audience Segments

Primary rule

Segment by product first. IMMUNE and SNOOZE subscribers retain at materially different rates (88% vs 74.5% at 90 days) and require different content. The document is explicit: do not apply a single onboarding flow to both.

SNOOZE subscribers Active subscription, SNOOZE product line Higher education burden at a higher price point. Must acknowledge variable results honestly. Lactium mechanism foregrounded — not lactoferrin.

Flow Architecture

Primary objective

Get subscribers to order 4. Across IMMUNE and SNOOZE, customers who reach their fourth order show materially higher long-term retention. Every flow is evaluated against this gate. The entire architecture replaces the existing structure — the current 90-day win-back trigger is outside the re-engagement window and must be paused before any new flows go live.

Suppression and Send-Frequency Logic

  • Post-win-back suppress: after final email in Flow 3 or 4, suppress from win-back flows for 90 days.
  • Active subscribers never receive win-back content. Explicitly stated in the document. Failure to suppress is flagged as a system error risk.
  • December 2025 cohort: active subscribers suppressed from the re-engagement flow entirely, regardless of cohort membership.
  • Existing 90-day win-back flow: must be paused before day 20–45 flows are activated — duplicate outreach risk.
  • Pre-cancel save (Flow 5): single SMS only alongside any email. Do not extend to a multi-message sequence.
  • Klaviyo metrics:read API: must be live in Growth Room before the 27-flow audit can confirm which flows already exist. This is a stated build blocker.
Gap

Campaign send-frequency cap and cross-flow fatigue logic are not specified in the document. No explicit rule governs contacts who are simultaneously eligible for multiple flows.

Tone and Messaging Principles

Universal principles

  • Science in plain language. Specified explicitly for onboarding and applies across all flows — not jargon-led, not promotional.
  • Set expectations explicitly. "Lactium's clinical trials show population-level effects, not universal response." This must be stated, not buried.
  • Honest framing over overclaiming. "Be explicit about who SNOOZE is for and what it can and cannot guarantee."
  • Founder voice is a performance asset. The document states: "structure it into creative, CRM sequences, and PR. It is not background brand texture."
  • Science and social proof are the primary conversion tools — not discount-first. "Never run out" framing is being tested against "20% off" as the primary subscription hook.
  • Urgency through science, not scarcity. Used in the win-back brief explicitly.

Content Pillars

1

Lactoferrin Category Authority

No dominant UK lactoferrin brand exists. 32 competitor domains are currently cited in AI search on lactoferrin and colostrum queries instead of Leapfrog. The window to establish the position is open now — in 12–18 months, better-resourced brands will arrive. Category authority cannot be bought after the category consolidates. Asset: 20+ lactoferrin RCTs — a content moat no competitor can quickly replicate.

2

Founder Authority

Stephanie Drax is positioned as "the person who brought clinical lactoferrin to the UK consumer market." The document states: "Founder voice is a performance asset — structure it into creative, CRM sequences, and PR. It is not background brand texture." Existing press coverage (Country & Town House, Vanity Fair, Daily Mail, Sheerluxe, Evening Standard) is underutilised — currently a logo bar, not an editorial programme.

3

Ingredient Science (Product-level)

Claim Specificity scores 20/100 across all product pages. The science estate exists but is not structured for AI retrieval, consumer trust, or conversion at the premium price threshold. Sub-pillars: IMMUNE/DAILY (lactoferrin — 20+ RCTs); SNOOZE (Lactium® — 6 RCTs; lactoferrin brand logic does not transfer to sleep; Lactium must be foregrounded); FOCUS (L-Theanine + Lactium dual mechanism; morning cortisol framing).

4

Category Intercept

Pages a category authority owns before the category consolidates. Currently owned by competitor domains in AI search. Named pages: 'What is lactoferrin?', 'Lactoferrin vs colostrum', 'Lactium vs Melatonin'. Top-of-funnel; feeds GEO citation; builds acquisition pipeline before category becomes crowded.

5

Social Proof and Outcomes

903 reviews at 4.6/5 are not surfaced prominently on primary PDPs. Review volume signals product efficacy at scale and should be structured for GEO attribution. IMMUNE: 14-order average, 88% 90-day retention are the proof points. SNOOZE: sleep outcome data; must be honest about timeline (2–4 week build).

Tone of Voice and Brand Constraints

Stated principles

  • Science-backed, consumer-accessible. "Ingredient science in plain language" — specified for email onboarding, applies equally to site content.
  • Honest, not promotional. "This framing is honest, coherent, and defensible" (used about the FOCUS/SNOOZE product architecture). Defensibility is a stated criterion for all claims.
  • Set expectations explicitly. Do not imply guaranteed results. SNOOZE copy must state that Lactium shows population-level effects, not universal response.
  • Premium, founder-led, press-credentialed. Selfridges listing, press coverage, and Stephanie's scientific authority are brand credibility signals — must be present in communications at the premium price threshold.
  • Messaging-first, not discount-first. The document argues against price reduction as a first intervention for both SNOOZE and FOCUS. Value case must be made before price is addressed.

Constraints

  • All specific product efficacy claims require Stephanie sign-off before publication or email deployment. Build blocker.
  • Do not claim universal efficacy. Population-level framing is mandatory.
  • The lactoferrin brand logic does not automatically transfer to SNOOZE. Lactium must be foregrounded in all SNOOZE content — do not anchor SNOOZE on lactoferrin.
Gap

A tone of voice document, brand voice guide, or content style guide is not referenced anywhere in the SOSTAC. No formal brand language system is documented.
