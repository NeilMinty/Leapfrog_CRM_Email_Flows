Source Material

Content briefs below are grounded in claims and copy already published on leapfrogremedies.com (homepage, IMMUNE and SNOOZE product pages, Lactoferrin and Lactium® science pages) — not invented or placeholder content. Because these claims are already live on the brand's own site, there's no need to develop or separately validate new efficacy claims. Final email-specific copy and tone should still get a brand sign-off pass before send, particularly anywhere a claim is reframed for a retention/urgency context rather than the sales context it currently sits in.

Key facts established from the site
  • Subscription offer: 20% off first three subscription orders, then 10% thereafter, plus "exclusive gifts on your subscription journey." This is the standing incentive referenced throughout Flows 5+6 — no separate one-off discount needed.
  • Free shipping: standard on all orders over £50 (not subscription-specific) — confirms it has no value as a win-back lever.
  • IMMUNE dosing: Emergency SOS (2 tablets/day, morning + evening, at first signs of a bug, for 1–5 days) vs Pulse Method™ (1 tablet/day, one month on/one month off, or two weeks on/two weeks off, for ongoing seasonal support). 250mg lactoferrin per tablet, plus zinc and vitamin C.
  • SNOOZE dosing: 1–2 tablets one hour before bed, or 1–2 tablets during night wake-ups. 150mg Lactium® per tablet, plus lactoferrin and vitamin B6 (P5P).
  • Lactium® clinical data: a 2022 study (300 subjects, 4 weeks) found sleep disorders improved for 77% of consumers and stress improved for 78% after 30 days of supplementation. A separate randomised crossover trial found increased total sleep time and sleep efficiency, with possible cumulative benefit with longer-term use.
  • Lactoferrin clinical data: 50+ clinical trials; described as anti-viral, anti-bacterial, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulatory. A meta-analysis of 25 studies found 200mg/day reduces systemic inflammation and may reduce respiratory tract infection incidence.

Why this sits outside Relo

Relo's predictive model runs on Shopify order history — it identifies patterns from prior reorder behaviour to time replenishment and subscription prompts. At M1, a subscriber has only one order on file, so there's no reorder history for the model to pattern-match against. Relo's value is structurally weakest at exactly the point Flow 3 targets.

What's more useful at M1 is broader cohort-level trend data already sitting in Growth Room — how similar subscribers (same product, same acquisition window) have historically behaved between day 20 and day 35, combined with individual engagement signal (email opens, science page visits) from Klaviyo. Both data sources sit outside Relo's integration (Shopify order data in, Klaviyo trigger out — no engagement data in), which is the underlying reason Flow 3 needs to be built natively rather than layered on Relo.

Klaviyo build requirements
  • Trigger condition: time-since-order = 20 days AND subscription status = active AND order count = 1
  • Critical exit logic: subscription status + order count must be checked at every send, not just entry. Any subscriber placing order 2 before day 33 must exit immediately — sending “delivery is on its way” post-renewal reads as a system error.
  • Coordinate suppression with Flow 1 (avoid duplicate day-25/day-33 content to the same subscriber)
  • Confirm Klaviyo flow exit conditions support live re-evaluation before build starts

5+6

OTP Win-Back

IMMUNE Priority · SNOOZE Secondary
Status — Migration away from Relo

Proposal is to migrate away from Relo entirely. Relo (Relay's repeat-revenue platform) works out by predictive analytics the exact day each customer is statistically most likely to want to replenish, then syncs that into Klaviyo flows and campaigns — its triggers are dynamic and customer-specific, generated by Relo's own model, not a fixed day-based rule visible or editable in Klaviyo. No one on the Leapfrog team can currently confirm what those rules are or how they're calculated, which makes an audit of “why is this firing too early” effectively a black box exercise with an external vendor.

Flows 5+6 replace this with fixed, day-based triggers (day 20/32 IMMUNE, day 32 SNOOZE) built natively in Klaviyo — fully transparent, fully owned, and editable by the team without vendor dependency. This trades Relo's per-customer predictive timing for simpler, auditable logic that the team can actually see and adjust. Once Flows 5+6 are live and stable, Relo can be switched off — deprecation timing to be agreed once the new flows are proven, but no audit of Relo's current rules is needed beforehand.

SNOOZE-Specific Content Note

Day 28/40 email must acknowledge sleep outcomes vary and take time — do not imply guaranteed results. Honest framing (“some feel it week 2, most by week 6”) reduces cancellation from unrealistic expectations more effectively than overclaiming. Consistent with SOSTAC finding: SNOOZE carries a higher education burden at a higher price point.

Discount Discipline — Pending Decision

The original strategy proposed a time-limited 10%-or-free-shipping offer at the final email only. Free shipping to UK addresses is already standard on orders over £50, so it has no incentive value as a win-back lever — this option is removed. The standing subscription offer (20% off first three orders, then 10% thereafter, plus subscription gifts) already exceeds a one-off 10% discount, so there's a reasonable case that no additional incentive is needed at email 3 — the existing offer just needs to be communicated clearly. Whether a separate one-off discount on top of this is introduced at email 3 is unconfirmed and needs sign-off before build. Until confirmed, all three emails convert on the standard subscribe-and-save offer, reorder logic, and outcome/value framing — no additional one-off incentive. If a discount is approved later, it slots into email 3 only, per the original discipline: emails 1–2 must convert on value and outcome alone.
